Accessible Gaming Through Standardized Subtitling: A Study on User Preferences and Inclusivity for Deaf and Hard of Hearing Individuals

Abstract

As the prevalence of video games rises, demands for enhanced quality, narrative variety, and increased accessibility have increased. This study emphasizes the importance of standardized subtitling in video games for all users, including those with disabilities. Beginning with analyzing current subtitling practices, this study reveals the lack of industry-wide standards. An exhaustive survey focused on text contrast, font size, line length, character identification, and color to collect data on the preferences of various demographic groups concerning subtitles. The data accumulated over four months from diverse participants revealed potential standards. This study provides an overview of the methodological approach, participant demographics, data acquisition and analysis, privacy safeguards, and survey question rationale. It concludes with an overview of the findings. It makes a case for future work on subtitling standards, emphasizing the urgency of such measures for more inclusive and accessible gaming experiences.
